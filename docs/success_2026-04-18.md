## U002 Pin Mapping Discovery

The original assumption from M5Stack documentation was:

- yellow wire `IR_TX` → `GPIO 10`
- white wire `IR_RX` → `GPIO 9`

**Actual verified mapping** (confirmed via pin sweep test):

- `GPIO 9` is the **TX** pin (LED fires on this pin)
- `GPIO 10` is the **RX** pin

This was discovered using the `Sweep U002` diagnostic mode, which blind-scans
candidate GPIO pins while the user observes the IR LED through a phone camera.

## U002 Status

`U002` is now **confirmed working** and part of the known-good path.

Observed behavior:

- `Beacon 38k` on `U002` TX (GPIO 9): **visible flash confirmed**
- Camera trigger on `U002` TX: **confirmed working**
- Effective range is comparable to the built-in emitter (~15cm)
- Loopback test on U002 TX→RX always times out because the TX LED and RX
  sensor face the same direction on the module; this is expected

## Dual TX Mode

The self-timer firmware now supports **dual simultaneous TX**:

- Both `BuiltIn` (GPIO 46) and `U002` (GPIO 9) fire the same payload at the
  same time
- This is controlled by `kDualTxEnabled = true` at compile time
- The UI backend selector (`BtnA` long press) designates the "primary" backend
  but both always fire when dual TX is enabled
- This provides two emission points for better spatial coverage

| Change | Before | After |
|---|---|---|

| Send repeats | 1 | 5 (50ms gap) |
| TX emitters | 1 (selected backend) | 2 (both fire simultaneously) |

## Outstanding Limitation

Effective trigger range remains approximately **15cm** even with dual TX and
increased power settings. Further range improvement would likely require
hardware changes:

- External transistor/MOSFET driver for higher LED current
- Higher-power IR LED module with narrower beam optic
- Reflector or lens to focus the IR beam
